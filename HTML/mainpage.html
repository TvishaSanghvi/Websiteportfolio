History of Design


History of Design as a class taught us about different art movements from 1850 to 2000 which then required us to make a product poster inspired by the movements.


Wine Posters

I created wine labels on different art movements and these are some of them- Romanticism, Cubism, Art Nouveau and Art Deco.

ROMANTICISM POSTER

The background print shows 'celebration of nature',
a key characteristic of Romanticism Period.
The painting is the middle is 'The Titan's Goblet'
by Thomas Cole, but edited in my favour.
Colour palette and whole vibe is inspired by the period.

CUBISM POSTER

The background is a painting by Piet Mondrain called
'The Gray Tree'. I created the wine glass in the middle
trying to show modern cubism art. The grapes on the
background tree symbolises the red grapes used to make the wine.

ART NOUVEAU POSTER

The background print is inspired by the flow
which is seen in many artworks of this period,
usually as hairs or on frame. The female in the
middle is from the Gismonda poster by Alphonse Mucha,
but tweaked in my favour. The font and colour
palette is inspired by the period.

ART DECO POSTER

The background print is inspired by the modern
art deco prints. The man illustrated is inspired by
the posters of the movement, specifically from a perfume
commercial. The font and colours are also movement inspired.

THE SELFIE NEWSLETTER

A four-sided A3 Newsletter that is based on finding my North star. First page- A name that relates to me. Sirius means the brighest 'star'. My name is Tvisha which
means 'Bright' and also Sirius is the godfather of Harry Potter, my favourite series. And a text
on a movie that influenced me in my younger years.
Middle spread- Answering few questions that ultimately helped me find me my north star (something I want to do
and strongly believe in). The polaroids depict memories and people that made me who I am today.
Last page- A self-clicked visual that shows something about me.
